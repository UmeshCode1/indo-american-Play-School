Our Curriculum Approach

At Indo American Play School, we've developed a unique curriculum that blends the best teaching methodologies from around the world while maintaining strong connections to cultural values.

Montessori-Inspired Learning

Child-led activities and self-paced learning in a carefully prepared environment

STEAM Integration

Science, Technology, Engineering, Arts and Mathematics woven throughout all programs

Multiple Intelligences

Activities designed to nurture different types of intelligences in every child

Bilingual Advantage

Instruction in both English and Hindi to promote bilingual development

Playgroup Program (Ages 2-3 years)

Our Playgroup program is designed to be a gentle introduction to the school environment, focusing on social skills, sensory exploration, and basic routines.

At this crucial developmental stage, children learn primarily through play and sensory experiences. Our nurturing environment encourages curiosity, language development, and emotional security.

What This Program Includes:

  • Sensory play activities to stimulate cognitive development
  • Language development through stories, rhymes, and music
  • Fine motor skills development through art and manipulation activities
  • Gross motor development through guided physical activities
  • Social skills through structured group interactions
  • Toilet training assistance and personal care routines
  • Introduction to basic colors, shapes, and numbers through play
Program Schedule Options

Half Day: 9:00 AM to 12:00 PM
Full Day: 9:00 AM to 2:00 PM
Extended Care Available: 8:00 AM to 3:00 PM

Nursery Program (Ages 3-4 years)

In the Nursery program, children begin more structured learning while maintaining a play-based approach. The curriculum focuses on developing independence, communication skills, and pre-literacy foundations.

At this stage, children are naturally curious and eager to learn. We channel this enthusiasm through engaging activities that introduce academic concepts in a fun, pressure-free environment.

What This Program Includes:

  • Pre-reading and pre-writing activities
  • Number concept development and basic counting
  • Science exploration through hands-on experiments
  • Creative arts including painting, music, and dramatic play
  • Physical education and movement activities
  • Social-emotional learning and conflict resolution
  • Bilingual instruction introducing both English and Hindi
Program Schedule Options

Half Day: 9:00 AM to 12:30 PM
Full Day: 9:00 AM to 2:30 PM
Extended Care Available: 8:00 AM to 3:00 PM

LKG Program (Ages 4-5 years)

Our Lower Kindergarten program builds upon previous foundations and introduces more structured learning experiences. Children develop early literacy and numeracy skills while continuing to learn through play and exploration.

At this age, children begin to show greater attention spans and problem-solving abilities. Our curriculum capitalizes on these developments while maintaining a balance between academics and creative exploration.

What This Program Includes:

  • Early reading with phonics and sight word introduction
  • Writing practice with proper pencil grip and letter formation
  • Mathematical concepts including patterns, sorting, and numbers 1-20
  • Science and environmental awareness activities
  • Cultural studies and celebration of festivals
  • Structured physical education and outdoor play
  • Technology introduction with age-appropriate educational apps
  • Second language development with emphasis on conversational skills
Program Schedule Options

Half Day: 9:00 AM to 1:00 PM
Full Day: 9:00 AM to 2:30 PM
Extended Care Available: 8:00 AM to 3:00 PM

UKG Program (Ages 5-6 years)

The Upper Kindergarten program prepares children for a smooth transition to primary school. While maintaining our play-based philosophy, this program introduces more academically rigorous content and independent work skills.

Children at this stage develop greater metacognitive abilities and can reflect on their own learning. Our curriculum encourages critical thinking, problem-solving, and the application of knowledge across different contexts.

What This Program Includes:

  • Reading comprehension and story analysis
  • Writing sentences and simple creative writing
  • Mathematical operations including addition, subtraction, and number concepts to 50
  • Scientific inquiry and hypothesis testing
  • Geography and cultural awareness studies
  • Coding fundamentals through unplugged activities
  • Public speaking and presentation skills
  • Self-regulation and executive function development
  • School readiness skills and homework habits
Program Schedule Options

Half Day: 9:00 AM to 1:00 PM
Full Day: 9:00 AM to 3:00 PM
Extended Care Available: 8:00 AM to 4:00 PM

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common questions about our programs

We maintain small class sizes to ensure personalized attention. Our teacher-to-student ratios are:
- Playgroup: 1:6
- Nursery: 1:8
- LKG: 1:10
- UKG: 1:12
Each class also has a teaching assistant in addition to the main teacher.

For our Playgroup program, we do not require children to be fully toilet trained upon enrollment. Our experienced staff will work with parents to support toilet training through our established routines. For Nursery and above, we expect children to be toilet trained, but we understand occasional accidents happen and handle them discreetly.

No, children do not need to be fluent in English before joining. Our bilingual approach supports children in developing both English and Hindi language skills. Teachers are trained to support language development regardless of a child's starting point, and we create an inclusive environment where all children feel comfortable communicating in either language.

We use a developmentally appropriate assessment approach that focuses on observing children's growth across multiple domains. Rather than traditional testing, our teachers maintain detailed portfolios of each child's work, document key milestones, and conduct regular observations. Parents receive comprehensive progress reports three times per year, and we hold parent-teacher conferences to discuss development and next steps.

Yes, we provide nutritious vegetarian snacks for all programs. For full-day programs, we also provide a balanced lunch. Our menu is carefully planned by a nutritionist and prepared in our hygienic kitchen. We accommodate dietary restrictions and allergies with advance notice. Parents can view the monthly menu in advance, and we encourage healthy eating habits as part of our curriculum.
